Building a static blog using pure HTML, CSS, and Markdown is a fantastic technical achievement. But once your site is live on **GitHub Pages**, how do you get people to actually read it? 

Let's break down the ultimate strategy for Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and monetizing with Google AdSense.

## Why Static Sites Win at SEO

Search engines like Google use "crawlers" to read your website. Modern websites built heavily with React or complex JavaScript can sometimes confuse these crawlers because the content isn't immediately visible.

Because our blog (Parth Posts) is generated into **pure HTML** by Eleventy, Google can read our content in milliseconds. Speed is a massive ranking factor in 2024.

## Key SEO Checklist

If you want your articles to rank, ensure you follow these rules on every post:

1. **Perfect Meta Titles:** Keep them under 60 characters. Make them catchy.
2. **Meta Descriptions:** Summarize the article in 150 characters. This is what shows up in Google search results!
3. **Semantic Tags:** Never use a `<h2>` just to make text big. Use `<h1>` for the main title, `<h2>` for sections, and `<h3>` for sub-sections.
4. **Keyword URL Slugs:** Notice how the URL for this post is `/seo-for-static-blogs/` instead of `/post-2/`? Keywords in URLs matter.

## Preparing for Google AdSense

Getting approved for AdSense requires a bit of patience. Google wants to see that your site is legitimate and valuable.

> **Pro Tip:** Do not apply for AdSense immediately! Wait until you have published at least **15 high-quality, long-form articles** (over 800 words each).

### Where to Place Your Ads
To maximize revenue without ruining the user experience, we have set up two prime locations in our CSS:
* **The Horizontal Banner:** Placed just below the hero section on the homepage.
* **The Sticky Vertical Sidebar:** When a user reads your blog on a desktop, this ad stays on the screen as they scroll down, generating high visibility.

Keep writing consistently, provide value to your readers, and the traffic (and revenue) will follow!
